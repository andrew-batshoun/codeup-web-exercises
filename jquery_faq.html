National Parks Facts

Yosemite National Park
Three of the 10 highest waterfalls in the world are located in Yosemite National Park in California.
Grand Canyon
The Grand Canyon, in Grand Canyon National Park in Arizona, is known as one of the Seven Natural Wonders of the World.
Sequoia National Park
California's Sequoia National Park is home to the largest living single-stem tree in the world, the wonderfully named General Sherman. The tree is approximately 275 feet tall and weighs approximately 1,900 metric tons.
Wrangell-St. Elais National Park
Located in Alaska, is the largest national park, covering 13,000 square miles or 13.2 million acres.
Great Smoky Mountain National Park
It is the most visited park with mor ethan 9 Million guests per year
Kobuk Valley national Park
It is the least visited park, it receives just 3000 visitors per year.
Carlsbad Caverns National Park
It is home to the Nation's deepest cave.
Glacier National Park
There are 25 active glaciers and more than 700 lakes of various sizes.
Mauna Loa Volcano in Hawaii Volcanoes National Park
The largest volcano on earth both in terms of volume and height above its base. It contains about 19,000 cubic miles of lava and rises more than 50,000 feet above its base, including the portion which is beneath the ocean.
Olympic National Park
Bears have not killed a single person in the park, but a mountain goat, which is not a native species to the region, killed a man in 2010.
